About Me

I am a 24-year-old Master in Informatics Engineering at the University of Coimbra, currently taking the Doctoral Program in Information Science and Technology at the University of Coimbra, working in the field of Cyber-Physical Systems.

Computers and technology in general have always been a very interesting part of my life, so I guess it was kind of natural for me ending up taking a Computer Science Degree.

Ever since I tried it for the first time, programming has been an activity that I've enjoyed a lot. I like to explore one idea/concept, give it my own vision and turn it into a complete, working, piece of software.

Besides software development, research has also proven to be a very exciting and challenging activity, which I have been experiencing since my third year at university. I enjoy studying topics that are relatively new to me and having the ability and freedom to explore my own ideas and theories. And with hard work (a lot) and a bit of luck (yes, sometimes we can all use a bit of luck), you will get to see the result of your work making a difference in other people's lives. And that makes it all worth.

I have experience in several programming languages - including C/C++, Java, Python, Matlab, ActionScript, SQL, HTML/CSS, JavaScript - and popular frameworks and libraries - jQuery, Django, AngularJS, Node.js, Struts2... I also have research experience in Artificial Intelligence, namely in the fields of Pattern Recognition and Evolutionary Computation (Supervised and Unsupervised Learning, Optimisation...). In the course of my most recent research works, I have also delved into the fields of context-aware and self-adaptive software systems.

I am always looking for new interesting challenges where I can learn and have fun at the same time.

Besides being a technology enthusiast, and therefore spending most of my time behind my laptop, I also try to get as much fresh air as I can, usually by playing some tennis at the local tennis club or going on an occasional run across the nearest park.
